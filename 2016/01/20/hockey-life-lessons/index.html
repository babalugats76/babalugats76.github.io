Hockey Life Lessons

January 20th, 2016

Summary

You can learn a lot from Canada’s national pastime: hockey. In this lesson, we focus on the many life lessons to be learned from studying the unique history and culture of hockey.

Resources

  • Handout: Hockey Life Lessons
  • USA Today: The Ivy Leaguer who fought his way to the NHL

Lesson Plan

  • Present lesson vocabulary
  • Build necessary background knowledge, e.g., what is hockey, popular figures, U.S. generations, etc.
  • Discuss 11 universal life lessons using demonstrable video evidence from the game of hockey.
  • Examine the personal odyssey of Sidney Crosby—from child prodigy to his present-day battle with concussions.
  • Watch excerpts from the popular film, Goon, a colorful parody of the role of enforcers in the NHL.
  • Read and answer comprehension questions from The Ivy Leaguer who fought his way to the NHL.
  • Reflect on fighting in the NHL: Is it mindless barbarism, a noble endeavor undertaken by modern-day knights, or something, somewhere in between?
